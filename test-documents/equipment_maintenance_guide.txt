EQUIPMENT MAINTENANCE GUIDE
Industrial Plant Operations Manual
Version 2.1 - January 2026

====================================
SECTION 1: SAFETY PROTOCOLS
====================================

1.1 GENERAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

All personnel must adhere to the following safety protocols before performing any maintenance work:

- Wear appropriate PPE (Personal Protective Equipment) including hard hat, safety glasses, steel-toed boots, and high-visibility vest
- Complete Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures for all equipment before maintenance
- Verify isolation of all energy sources (electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic, thermal)
- Use fall protection when working at heights above 6 feet
- Maintain clear communication with control room operators
- Follow confined space entry procedures where applicable

1.2 EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

In case of emergency:
1. Immediately notify control room (Ext. 911)
2. Activate emergency stop if safe to do so
3. Evacuate to designated assembly points
4. Do not attempt to fight fires unless trained and safe to do so
5. Account for all team members at assembly point

SECTION 2: CONVEYOR SYSTEMS
====================================

2.1 PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE

Daily Inspections (8:00 AM shift start):
- Check belt tension and alignment
- Inspect for material buildup on rollers
- Verify emergency stop functionality
- Check for unusual noise or vibration
- Inspect belt surface for damage or wear

Weekly Maintenance (Every Monday):
- Lubricate all bearings with NLGI Grade 2 grease
- Check drive belt tension
- Inspect and clean belt cleaners
- Test all safety interlocks
- Document all findings in maintenance log

Monthly Maintenance (First Monday of month):
- Complete alignment check with laser alignment tool
- Replace worn rollers (minimum 5mm wear)
- Check motor amperage draw
- Inspect electrical connections
- Test variable frequency drive (VFD) parameters

Annual Overhaul:
- Complete belt replacement if wear exceeds 15%
- Replace all bearings and seals
- Calibrate load cells and speed sensors
- Complete structural inspection
- Update maintenance records

2.2 COMMON ISSUES AND TROUBLESHOOTING

Issue: Belt tracking to one side
Causes:
- Misaligned idlers
- Material buildup on rollers
- Uneven belt tension
- Structural damage

Solutions:
- Adjust idler positions
- Clean rollers and belt surface
- Check tension across entire belt width
- Inspect structure for damage or settling

Issue: Motor overheating
Causes:
- Overloading beyond rated capacity
- Inadequate ventilation
- Bearing failure
- Electrical faults

Solutions:
- Reduce load or increase belt speed
- Clean motor cooling fins
- Replace bearings if temperature exceeds 80°C
- Check motor current draw and insulation resistance

SECTION 3: PUMP SYSTEMS
====================================

3.1 CENTRIFUGAL PUMP MAINTENANCE

Pre-Startup Checklist:
- Verify pump and motor alignment
- Check oil level in bearing housing
- Ensure suction valve is open
- Prime pump if required
- Check seal flush system

Operating Parameters:
- Flow rate: 500-1000 GPM
- Discharge pressure: 50-75 PSI
- Motor current: <30 amps
- Bearing temperature: <75°C
- Vibration: <0.3 in/sec

Routine Maintenance:
- Daily: Check for leaks, noise, and vibration
- Weekly: Check oil level and seal condition
- Monthly: Monitor performance (flow vs. pressure)
- Quarterly: Complete vibration analysis
- Annual: Replace seals and bearings

3.2 POSITIVE DISPLACEMENT PUMPS

Common Applications:
- Chemical dosing
- High-viscosity fluids
- Precise metering applications

Key Maintenance Points:
- Check packing adjustment weekly
- Monitor suction and discharge pressures
- Verify stroke length and frequency
- Inspect valves and seats monthly
- Replace diaphragms annually (or per manufacturer recommendation)

====================================
SECTION 4: HEAT EXCHANGERS
====================================

4.1 SHELL AND TUBE HEAT EXCHANGERS

Performance Monitoring:
- Temperature differential across both streams
- Pressure drop (tube side and shell side)
- Flow rates and velocities
- Heat transfer coefficient calculation

Fouling Indicators:
- Increased pressure drop (>20% from baseline)
- Reduced heat transfer efficiency
- Temperature approach narrowing
- Increased pumping costs

Cleaning Procedures:
1. Isolate and drain exchanger
2. Remove tube bundle (if accessible)
3. High-pressure water jet cleaning (3000-5000 PSI)
4. Chemical cleaning for stubborn deposits
5. Inspect tubes for corrosion or erosion
6. Reinstall and test for leaks

Preventive Measures:
- Install strainers on inlet streams
- Monitor water chemistry (pH, hardness, chlorides)
- Use appropriate chemical treatments
- Schedule cleaning before efficiency drops below 80%

====================================
SECTION 5: COMPRESSED AIR SYSTEMS
====================================

5.1 AIR COMPRESSOR MAINTENANCE

Daily Checks:
- Check oil level in compressor sump
- Drain receiver tank condensate
- Record discharge temperature
- Listen for unusual noise
- Check for air leaks

Weekly Maintenance:
- Clean air intake filter
- Check belt tension (belt-driven units)
- Test safety valve operation
- Check for oil leaks
- Monitor operating hours

Monthly Tasks:
- Replace air/oil separator element
- Change oil filter
- Check motor bearing temperature
- Test moisture separator
- Verify pressure switch settings

Annual Service:
- Complete oil change (synthetic oil recommended)
- Replace inlet valve and unloader valve
- Inspect airend for wear
- Calibrate pressure gauges
- Complete comprehensive leak survey

5.2 AIR LEAK DETECTION

Common Leak Points:
- Hose connections
- Quick disconnect fittings
- Threaded pipe joints
- Valve packing glands
- Flange gaskets

Detection Methods:
- Ultrasonic leak detector (most effective)
- Soapy water solution
- Acoustic monitoring
- Annual professional audit

Cost Impact:
- 1/8" leak @ 100 PSI = $2,000/year
- 1/4" leak @ 100 PSI = $8,000/year
- 3/8" leak @ 100 PSI = $18,000/year
- 1/2" leak @ 100 PSI = $32,000/year

====================================
SECTION 6: ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS
====================================

6.1 MOTOR CONTROL CENTERS (MCC)

Safety Requirements:
- Only qualified electricians permitted
- Arc flash hazard assessment required
- Appropriate PPE based on incident energy level
- Live work permit required for energized work

Routine Inspections:
- Check for signs of overheating
- Inspect connections for corrosion
- Test overload protection
- Verify ground fault protection
- Check contactor condition

Thermal Imaging:
- Scan all MCC buckets quarterly
- Temperature rise >20°C above ambient requires investigation
- Compare similar loads for consistency
- Document all findings with infrared camera

6.2 VARIABLE FREQUENCY DRIVES (VFD)

Maintenance Schedule:
- Monthly: Clean cooling fans and heatsinks
- Quarterly: Check DC bus capacitor voltage
- Semi-annually: Tighten all power connections
- Annually: Check surge protection devices
- Every 5 years: Replace cooling fans and capacitors

Common Faults:
- Overtemperature: Clean heatsink, check ventilation
- Overcurrent: Check motor and cable insulation
- Ground fault: Check motor and cable connections
- Communication fault: Check network connections

====================================
SECTION 7: INSTRUMENTATION
====================================

7.1 FLOW METERS

Calibration Requirements:
- Magnetic flowmeters: Annual verification
- Coriolis meters: Annual verification
- Ultrasonic meters: Every 6 months
- Differential pressure meters: Every 3 months

Installation Requirements:
- Minimum 5D straight run upstream, 2D downstream
- Avoid elbows, valves, and reducers nearby
- Ensure full pipe during operation
- Proper grounding essential for magnetic meters

7.2 TEMPERATURE SENSORS

Types and Applications:
- RTD: High accuracy (±0.1°C), -200 to 600°C
- Thermocouple: Wide range (-200 to 2000°C), lower accuracy
- Thermistor: High sensitivity, limited range
- Infrared: Non-contact, surface temperature

Calibration Schedule:
- Process critical: Quarterly
- Non-critical: Annually
- After replacement: Before returning to service

SECTION 8: LUBRICATION
====================================

8.1 LUBRICANT SELECTION

Grease Applications:
- NLGI Grade 2: Most electric motors, general bearings
- NLGI Grade 1: High-speed applications, cold environments
- NLGI Grade 3: Vertical shafts, high-temperature applications

Oil Applications:
- ISO VG 32: Hydraulic systems, compressors
- ISO VG 46: Gearboxes, medium-speed bearings
- ISO VG 68: Heavy-duty gearboxes, slow-speed bearings

8.2 LUBRICATION SCHEDULES

Electric Motors:
- Sleeve bearings: Oil bath, check monthly
- Ball bearings: Greased, relubricate per manufacturer schedule
- Large motors (>100 HP): Monthly checks, annual oil change

Pumps:
- Centrifugal pumps: Check oil weekly, change quarterly
- Positive displacement: Follow manufacturer recommendations
- Submersible pumps: Oil-filled, annual change

Gearboxes:
- Check oil level daily
- Change oil annually or every 5000 operating hours
- Use synthetic oil for extended drain intervals
- Sample oil for analysis quarterly
