SECTION 1: LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO)
====================================

1.1 PURPOSE

Lockout/Tagout procedures protect employees from the unexpected energization or startup of machinery and equipment, or the release of hazardous energy during service and maintenance activities.

1.2 SCOPE

This procedure applies to all employees who perform service or maintenance on machines and equipment where unexpected energization could cause injury.

1.3 DEFINITIONS

Affected Employee: An employee whose job requires them to work in an area where servicing or maintenance is being performed under lockout or tagout.

Authorized Employee: A person who locks out or tags out machines or equipment to perform servicing or maintenance. An authorized employee has received training and demonstrated competence.

Energy Isolating Device: A mechanical device that physically prevents transmission or release of energy (e.g., circuit breaker, disconnect switch, valve).

Lockout: Placement of a lock on an energy isolating device indicating that the energy isolating device shall not be operated.

Tagout: Placement of a tag on an energy isolating device indicating that the energy isolating device shall not be operated.

1.4 LOTO PROCEDURE STEPS

Preparation:
1. Identify all energy sources (electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical, thermal, stored energy)
2. Obtain necessary locks, tags, and blocking devices
3. Notify affected employees that LOTO will be implemented

Shutdown:
4. Shut down the equipment using normal stopping procedure
5. Operate the main disconnect switch or valve to isolate all energy sources

Isolation:
6. Isolate all energy sources using energy isolating devices
7. Apply personal locks and tags to each energy isolating device
8. Each worker must apply their own lock

Verification:
9. Attempt to restart the equipment to verify isolation
10. Use test equipment to verify zero energy state
11. Release any stored or residual energy
12. Verify that all energy sources are isolated

Work Performance:
13. Perform the required maintenance or service work
14. Keep all locks and tags in place throughout the work
15. Never remove another person's lock or tag

Release from LOTO:
16. Verify all tools and equipment are removed
17. Verify all guards and safety devices are reinstalled
18. Ensure all employees are clear of the equipment
19. Remove locks and tags (only by the person who applied them)
20. Notify affected employees that LOTO is removed
21. Restore energy and test equipment operation

1.5 LOCKOUT DEVICE REQUIREMENTS

Locks:
- Standardized by color (red) and design
- Uniquely keyed
- Durable and substantial
- Identified with employee name and date
- Never used for any other purpose

Tags:
- Standardized design and format
- Durable, weather-resistant
- Attached with non-reusable fastener
- Include: DANGER message, employee name, date, reason for lockout
- Include contact information

1.6 PERIODIC INSPECTION

Annual inspection of LOTO procedures required:
- Conducted by authorized employee other than the one using the procedure
- Correct any deficiencies identified
- Document inspection results
- Retrain if deviations observed

1.7 TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

Authorized employees must receive training on:
- Recognition of hazardous energy sources
- Types and magnitudes of energy in the workplace
- Methods and means of energy isolation and control
- LOTO procedures specific to equipment
- Limitations of tags (if used alone)

Retraining required:
- Annually
- When procedures change
- When deficiencies are identified
- When new equipment is introduced

SECTION 2: CONFINED SPACE ENTRY
====================================

2.1 DEFINITION

A confined space has:
1. Limited or restricted means of entry or exit
2. Is large enough for an employee to enter
3. Is not designed for continuous employee occupancy

A PERMIT-REQUIRED confined space has one or more of:
1. Contains or has potential to contain a hazardous atmosphere
2. Contains material that could engulf the entrant
3. Has internal configuration that could trap or asphyxiate entrant
4. Contains any other recognized serious safety or health hazard

2.2 PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

Before entry into a permit-required confined space:
- Complete confined space entry permit
- Test atmosphere for oxygen, flammability, and toxicity
- Verify ventilation and communication
- Assign attendant outside the space
- Ensure rescue equipment and team available
- Brief all personnel on hazards and procedures

2.3 ATMOSPHERE TESTING

Required tests (in order):
1. Oxygen content (19.5-23.5% required)
2. Flammability (LEL <10% required)
3. Toxic gases (H2S, CO, etc. - varies by space)

Testing frequency:
- Before entry
- Continuous monitoring during entry
- After any change in conditions

2.4 ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

Entry Supervisor:
- Verifies permit requirements are met
- Authorizes entry
- Terminates entry if conditions change

Authorized Entrant:
- Uses required PPE
- Communicates with attendant
- Exits immediately if ordered or if danger perceived

Attendant:
- Maintains accurate headcount
- Remains outside during entry
- Communicates with entrants
- Orders evacuation if conditions change
- Summons rescue if needed
- Never leaves post until properly relieved

2.5 RESCUE PROCEDURES

Non-entry rescue preferred:
- Use retrieval line attached to entrant
- Mechanical lifting device if vertical entry >5 feet
- Attendant can retrieve entrant without entering

Entry rescue (if required):
- Only trained rescue personnel may enter
- Minimum 2-person rescue team
- Full PPE and SCBA required
- Practice drills conducted annually

SECTION 3: HOT WORK PERMIT
====================================

3.1 SCOPE

Hot work includes:
- Welding, cutting, and brazing
- Grinding and abrasive cutting
- Soldering and torch work
- Any operation producing sparks, flames, or heat

3.2 PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

Hot work permit required for all hot work except:
- Designated welding shops
- Construction sites with no fire hazard
- Areas specifically approved for hot work

Permit valid for:
- One shift only
- Specific location and equipment
- Specific time period

3.3 PRE-WORK CHECKLIST

Before issuing permit:
- Remove all flammable materials (35 feet radius)
- Protect combustibles that cannot be removed
- Cover floor and wall openings
- Test atmosphere for flammability
- Provide fire extinguisher (minimum 20 lb ABC)
- Assign fire watch
- Notify affected personnel

3.4 FIRE WATCH REQUIREMENTS

Fire watch required:
- During hot work operations
- 30 minutes after hot work ends

Fire watch duties:
- Watch for fires in all exposed areas
- Attempt to extinguish incipient fires
- Alert others and sound alarm if fire spreads
- Know how to use fire extinguisher
- Remain in area until relieved

3.5 RESPONSIBILITIES

Permit Authorizer:
- Inspects work area
- Verifies all precautions taken
- Issues permit
- Conducts final inspection

Fire Watch:
- Positioned to see all exposed areas
- Trained in fire extinguisher use
- Has immediate access to extinguisher
- Knows how to activate fire alarm

SECTION 4: FALL PROTECTION
====================================

4.1 SCOPE

Fall protection required when working at heights of:
- 4 feet or more in general industry
- 6 feet or more in construction
- Any height above dangerous equipment

4.2 PROTECTION METHODS

Hierarchy of controls:
1. Eliminate the hazard (work at ground level)
2. Guardrails and covers (passive protection)
3. Personal fall arrest systems (active protection)
4. Controlled access zones
5. Safety monitoring systems

4.3 GUARDRAIL SYSTEMS

Specifications:
- Top rail: 39-45 inches above walking/working surface
- Mid-rail: Midway between top rail and surface
- Toe board: 4 inches high (minimum)
- Capable of withstanding 200 pounds force

4.4 PERSONAL FALL ARREST SYSTEMS

Components:
- Anchorage point (capable of 5000 pounds)
- Body harness (full body, not body belt)
- Connecting device (lanyard or self-retracting lifeline)
- Shock absorber to limit fall arrest force to 1800 pounds

Inspection:
- Before each use
- By competent person annually
- Remove from service if any damage observed

Training required on:
- Proper donning of harness
- Selection of anchor points
- Maximum free fall distance
- Swing fall hazards
- Rescue procedures

SECTION 5: ELECTRICAL SAFETY
====================================

5.1 QUALIFIED PERSON REQUIREMENTS

Only qualified persons may work on or near exposed energized parts. Qualification requires:
- Training on electrical hazards
- Knowledge of safe work practices
- Skills to determine safe approach distances
- Training on emergency procedures

5.2 ARC FLASH PROTECTION

Hazard analysis required:
- Determine available fault current
- Calculate arc flash boundary
- Determine incident energy level
- Select appropriate PPE

PPE categories:
- Category 1: 4 cal/cm² minimum
- Category 2: 8 cal/cm² minimum
- Category 3: 25 cal/cm² minimum
- Category 4: 40 cal/cm² minimum

5.3 APPROACH BOUNDARIES

Limited approach boundary:
- Only qualified persons may enter
- Unqualified persons must stay outside

Restricted approach boundary:
- Qualified persons only
- Must use insulated tools
- PPE required

Prohibited approach boundary:
- Treated same as contact with energized part
- Additional precautions required

SECTION 6: CHEMICAL HANDLING
====================================

6.1 HAZARD COMMUNICATION

Requirements:
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) available for all chemicals
- Container labeling with identity and hazard warnings
- Employee training on hazards and protective measures
- Written hazard communication program

6.2 PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT

Selection based on:
- Chemical properties (corrosive, toxic, reactive)
- Exposure route (inhalation, skin contact, ingestion)
- Concentration and duration of exposure
- Work conditions (temperature, ventilation)

Common PPE:
- Chemical splash goggles
- Face shield (for corrosives)
- Chemical-resistant gloves
- Chemical-resistant apron or suit
- Respirator (if ventilation inadequate)

6.3 SPILL RESPONSE

Small spill (<1 gallon):
- Alert others in immediate area
- Don appropriate PPE
- Contain spill with absorbent
- Collect and dispose properly
- Clean area with appropriate solvent

Large spill or unknown material:
- Evacuate immediate area
- Activate emergency response
- Do not attempt cleanup
- Wait for trained responders

SECTION 7: EMERGENCY RESPONSE
====================================

7.1 EMERGENCY CONTACTS

Plant Emergency: Dial 911 or Ext. 911
Fire Department: 911
Ambulance: 911
Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
Environmental Spill: (State environmental agency number)

7.2 EMERGENCY ALARMS

Fire: Continuous horn
Evacuation: Intermittent horn
Shelter-in-place: Verbal announcement

7.3 EVACUATION PROCEDURES

Upon hearing evacuation alarm:
1. Stop work safely
2. Shut down equipment if time permits
3. Do not gather personal belongings
4. Proceed to nearest exit
5. Go to designated assembly point
6. Report to supervisor for headcount
7. Do not re-enter until "all clear" given

7.4 ASSEMBLY POINTS

- Assembly Point A: North parking lot (Production Building)
- Assembly Point B: South lawn (Office Building)
- Assembly Point C: East gate (Warehouse)

SECTION 8: PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT
====================================

8.1 HAZARD ASSESSMENT

Required for each work area:
- Identify hazards present
- Select appropriate PPE
- Communicate selection to affected employees
- Train on proper use and care

8.2 PPE STANDARDS

Hard hats: ANSI Z89.1
Safety glasses: ANSI Z87.1
Safety shoes: ASTM F2413
Gloves: EN 388 or ANSI/ISEA 105
Hearing protection: ANSI S3.19

8.3 PPE REQUIREMENTS BY AREA

Production Floor:
- Hard hat
- Safety glasses
- Steel-toed shoes
- High-visibility vest
- Hearing protection (designated areas)

Chemical Handling:
- All above plus:
- Chemical splash goggles
- Chemical-resistant gloves
- Face shield (corrosives)

Welding Area:
- All above plus:
- Welding helmet with appropriate shade
- Welding gloves
- Leather apron

SECTION 9: INCIDENT REPORTING
====================================

9.1 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

All incidents must be reported, including:
- Injuries (regardless of severity)
- Near misses
- Property damage
- Environmental releases
- Fires and explosions
- Vehicle accidents

9.2 IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

For any incident:
1. Ensure scene is safe
2. Provide first aid if trained
3. Secure the area
4. Notify supervisor immediately
5. Do not disturb evidence (unless safety hazard)
6. Complete incident report within 24 hours

9.3 INVESTIGATION

All incidents investigated to:
- Determine root cause
- Identify corrective actions
- Prevent recurrence
- Document lessons learned

SECTION 10: FIRE SAFETY
====================================

10.1 FIRE PREVENTION

Good housekeeping:
- Keep work areas clean
- Dispose of oily rags properly
- Store flammables in approved cabinets
- Do not block fire exits or equipment

Hot work precautions:
- Obtain permit before starting
- Remove or protect combustibles
- Provide fire watch
- Keep extinguisher nearby

10.2 FIRE EXTINGUISHER USE

P.A.S.S. method:
- Pull the pin
- Aim at base of fire
- Squeeze the handle
- Sweep side to side

Only attempt to extinguish:
- Small fires (wastebasket size or smaller)
- If trained and confident
- If safe escape route exists
- If correct extinguisher is available

10.3 FIRE EXTINGUISHER TYPES

Class A: Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper, cloth)
Class B: Flammable liquids (oil, grease, solvents)
Class C: Electrical equipment
Class D: Combustible metals
Class K: Cooking oils and fats
