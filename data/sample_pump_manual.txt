## 1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATIONS

### 1.1 Performance Specifications
- **Type**: Single-stage, end-suction centrifugal pump
- **Design**: API 610 / ISO 13709 compliant
- **Rated Flow**: 500 GPM (1,893 L/min)
- **Design Pressure**: 150 PSI (10.3 bar)
- **Maximum Pressure**: 200 PSI (13.8 bar)
- **Design Temperature**: 80°C (176°F)
- **Maximum Temperature**: 95°C (203°F)
- **Suction Size**: 6 inches (150 mm)
- **Discharge Size**: 4 inches (100 mm)

- **Motor Power**: 75 HP (56 kW)

### 1.2 Materials of Construction
- **Casing**: Cast Steel (ASTM A216 WCB)

### 1.3 Operating Limits

## 2. INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Foundation
- Concrete foundation minimum 6 inches thick
- Foundation bolts: 3/4 inch diameter, Grade 8
- Torque specification: **150 ft-lbs**
- Use anti-seize compound on threads
- Level pump within 0.002 inches per foot

### 2.2 Piping
- Support all piping independently
- Maintain alignment within 0.005 inches
- Install suction strainer (40 mesh minimum)
- Provide isolation valves on suction and discharge
- Install pressure gauges on both sides
- Ensure proper pipe stress analysis completed

### 2.3 Electrical
- Motor: 460V, 3-phase, 60 Hz
- Starter: NEMA Size 5, with overload protection
- Variable Frequency Drive: ABB ACS880 (optional)
- Power cable: 3/C #2 AWG copper
- Grounding: Separate ground wire, #6 AWG minimum
- Conduit: 2-inch rigid steel

## 3. STARTUP PROCEDURE

### 3.1 Pre-Start Checks
1. Verify all installation complete and torqued properly
2. Check alignment (see Section 6.2)
3. Rotate shaft by hand - should turn freely
4. Verify lubrication (see Section 5.1)
5. Check seal flush connections
6. Verify motor rotation direction
7. Ensure suction and discharge valves are in correct position
8. Check all instrumentation for proper readings

### 3.2 Initial Startup
1. Open suction isolation valve fully
2. Crack discharge valve slightly (10-20% open)
3. Start pump and immediately verify flow
4. Gradually open discharge valve to desired flow
5. Monitor for unusual noise or vibration
6. Check seal for leakage (some weeping is normal initially)
7. Verify motor current within nameplate limits
8. Record baseline vibration and temperature readings

### 3.3 Normal Operation
- Maintain flow between 100-600 GPM
- Keep suction pressure above NPSH + 5 ft safety margin
- Monitor discharge pressure
- Check bearing temperature hourly (first 24 hours)
- Listen for unusual noises
- Check seal for excessive leakage

---

## 4. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE

### 4.1 Normal Shutdown
1. Slowly close discharge valve
2. Stop motor
3. Close suction valve
4. Allow pump to cool before maintenance

### 4.2 Emergency Shutdown
1. Press emergency stop button
2. Close both suction and discharge valves
3. Investigate cause before restart
4. Document incident in logbook

## 5. MAINTENANCE

### 5.1 Lubrication Schedule

#### Bearings
- **Lubricant**: Mobilgrease XHP 222
- **Quantity**: 2 oz per bearing
- **Frequency**: Every 2,000 operating hours or 6 months
- **Procedure**:
  1. Clean grease fittings
  2. Add grease while pump is running
  3. Purge old grease until fresh grease appears
  4. Wipe excess grease

#### Mechanical Seal
- **Flush Fluid**: Clean, filtered water
- **Pressure**: 10 PSI above pump discharge
- **Flow**: 1-2 GPM
- **Temperature**: Below 60°C

### 5.2 Inspection Schedule

#### Daily (During Operation)
- Visual inspection for leaks
- Listen for unusual noises
- Check vibration by hand
- Verify temperatures normal
- Check lubrication oil level
- Inspect seal for leakage

#### Weekly
- Record vibration readings
- Record bearing temperatures
- Check motor current
- Inspect coupling for wear
- Clean pump exterior

#### Monthly
- Check alignment (while running)
- Inspect foundation bolts
- Check seal flush system
- Inspect suction strainer
- Review trend data

#### Quarterly
- Full vibration analysis
- Thermographic inspection
- Ultrasonic seal inspection
- Sample and analyze lubricant
- Check pump performance curve

#### Annually
- Overhaul mechanical seal
- Replace bearings
- Check impeller for erosion
- Inspect internal clearances
- Pressure test casing
- Megger test motor
- Update documentation

### 5.3 Mechanical Seal Replacement

**Tools Required**:
- Torque wrench (0-200 ft-lbs)
- Seal installation tool
- Feeler gauges
- Micrometer
- Soft hammer
- Clean rags
- Safety glasses and gloves

**Parts Required**:
- Mechanical seal assembly (PN: JC-21-125)
- O-rings (PN: VIT-125-75)
- Gaskets (PN: SW-125-316G)
- Anti-seize compound
- Seal faces cleaner

**Procedure**:
1. **Preparation** (30 minutes)
   - Isolate pump and lock out power (LOTO)
   - Drain pump completely
   - Vent pressure to atmosphere
   - Disconnect seal flush piping
   - Remove coupling guard

2. **Disassembly** (1 hour)
   - Disconnect coupling
   - Remove bearing housing bolts (Torque: 45 ft-lbs)
   - Carefully slide back bearing housing
   - Remove mechanical seal gland bolts (Torque: 35 ft-lbs)
   - Remove old seal assembly
   - Clean all mating surfaces

3. **Inspection** (30 minutes)
   - Inspect shaft for wear or corrosion
   - Check shaft runout (max 0.002 inches)
   - Inspect seal chamber for erosion
   - Measure shaft diameter (should be 2.500 ± 0.002 inches)
   - Check O-ring grooves for damage

4. **Installation** (1 hour)
   - Clean new seal faces with approved solvent
   - Lubricate O-rings with seal manufacturer's lubricant
   - Install new seal assembly per manufacturer's instructions
   - **CRITICAL**: Set seal face gap to 0.020 inches using feeler gauge
   - Install seal gland with new gasket
   - **Torque gland bolts to 35 ft-lbs in star pattern**
   - Reconnect seal flush piping

5. **Reassembly** (1 hour)
   - Slide bearing housing back into position
   - **Torque bearing housing bolts to 45 ft-lbs**
   - Reconnect coupling
   - Install coupling guard
   - Remove LOTO
   - Perform pre-start checks

6. **Testing** (30 minutes)
   - Start seal flush system
   - Verify no leaks
   - Start pump and run for 5 minutes
   - Check for any weeping (up to 10 drops/hour is acceptable initially)
   - Monitor for 1 hour, checking every 15 minutes
   - Record seal leakage rate

**Total Time**: Approximately 4-5 hours

**Safety Notes**:
- Always follow LOTO procedures
- Wear appropriate PPE
- Use proper lifting techniques
- Dispose of old seals properly
- Never operate pump with damaged seal

## 6. TROUBLESHOOTING

### 6.1 Common Problems

#### Problem: Pump Won't Start
**Possible Causes**:
- Electrical fault
- Motor overload tripped
- Coupling misaligned
- Shaft seized

**Solutions**:
1. Check electrical connections
2. Reset overload
3. Check alignment (see 6.2)
4. Manually rotate shaft - should turn freely
5. Call maintenance if still issues

#### Problem: Low Flow or Pressure
**Possible Causes**:
- Suction strainer clogged
- Air in suction line
- Impeller worn or damaged
- Wrong rotation direction
- Cavitation

**Solutions**:
1. Clean suction strainer
2. Vent air from system
3. Inspect impeller, replace if needed
4. Verify rotation (should be clockwise when viewed from motor)
5. Increase suction pressure or reduce flow

#### Problem: Excessive Vibration
**Acceptable Vibration**: < 0.3 inches/sec RMS

**Possible Causes**:
- Misalignment
- Unbalanced impeller
- Worn bearings
- Cavitation
- Piping strain
- Loose foundation bolts

**Solutions**:
1. **If > 0.5 in/sec: STOP PUMP IMMEDIATELY**
2. Check alignment (tolerance: 0.002 inches)
3. Check bearing condition
4. Balance or replace impeller
5. Inspect piping support
6. Tighten foundation bolts to 150 ft-lbs

#### Problem: Seal Leakage
**Acceptable Leakage**: < 10 drops/hour after break-in

**Possible Causes**:
- Seal faces damaged
- Seal improperly installed
- Wrong seal face gap
- Seal flush system failure
- Excessive vibration
- Chemical attack

**Solutions**:
1. Check seal flush pressure (should be 10 PSI above discharge)
2. Check seal flush flow (should be 1-2 GPM)
3. Inspect for vibration
4. Replace seal if leakage > 1 drop/minute after 24 hours

#### Problem: High Bearing Temperature
**Normal Temperature**: 140-160°F (60-71°C)
**Alarm**: 180°F (82°C)
**Trip**: 200°F (93°C)

**Possible Causes**:
- Over-greasing
- Under-greasing
- Wrong lubricant
- Bearing failure
- Misalignment

**Solutions**:
1. Check grease level
2. Verify correct lubricant (Mobilgrease XHP 222)
3. Check alignment
4. If > 180°F: Shutdown and replace bearings

## 7. ALIGNMENT PROCEDURE

### 7.1 Tools Required
- Dial indicators (0.001 inch resolution)
- Magnetic bases
- Feeler gauges
- Soft face hammer
- Shims (0.001, 0.005, 0.010, 0.020 inch)
- Torque wrench

### 7.2 Alignment Tolerance
- **Parallel Misalignment**: < 0.002 inches TIR
- **Angular Misalignment**: < 0.001 inches per inch of diameter
- **Axial Clearance**: 0.010 - 0.020 inches

### 7.3 Procedure
1. Remove coupling guard
2. Separate coupling halves
3. Mount dial indicators for parallel and angular readings
4. Rotate shafts together, record readings every 90°
5. Calculate offset and angularity
6. Add or remove shims as needed
7. Recheck alignment
8. Repeat until within tolerance
9. Torque mounting bolts to 150 ft-lbs
10. Final alignment check
11. Reconnect coupling
12. Document readings in maintenance log

## 9. SAFETY INFORMATION

### 9.1 Hazards
- **Rotating Equipment**: Keep hands and clothing away
- **High Pressure**: Can cause injection injuries
- **Hot Surfaces**: Pump can reach 95°C
- **Electrical**: 460V can be fatal
- **Confined Space**: Pump internals (during maintenance)

### 9.2 Required PPE
- Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1)
- Steel-toed boots
- Hard hat
- Hearing protection (>85 dBA)
- Heat-resistant gloves (during operation)
- Chemical-resistant gloves (during maintenance)

### 9.3 Lockout/Tagout
- Always follow plant LOTO procedures
- Lock out electrical power
- Tag out isolation valves
- Verify zero energy state
- Test before touching

### 9.4 Emergency Procedures
- **Fire**: Use CO2 or dry chemical extinguisher
- **Leak**: Evacuate area, shut down remotely if possible
- **Injury**: Call emergency services, provide first aid
- **Spill**: Follow plant spill response plan
